NBA Performance Predictor

Tools: Python

The aim of this study was to gain an understanding of which basketball statistics were predictive of team success in the NBA, assisting internal strategic planning and probabilistic modeling for franchises and various stakeholders of the league. Dimensionality reduction techniques like Principal Component Analysis and Linear Discriminant Analysis were employed to highlight the most impactful metrics on team success. From this, it was found that three-point percentage, turnover percentage, and rebounding statistics were most influential on the variability of performance.

Additionally, seasonal performance predictability was strengthened by designing clustering models to accurately group teams in four-tiered performance classes based on team statistics. This evaluation provided strong predictive accuracy for projections, realizing 89% accuracy in predicted tier classification.

Achievements

  • Highlighted most impactful metrics on team success, such as three-point percentage and rebounding statistics, employing dimensionality reduction techniques like Principal Component Analysis and Linear Discriminant Analysis.
  • Strengthened seasonal performance predictability by designing clustering models to accurately group teams in four-tiered performance classes based on team statistics, realizing 89% accuracy in predicted tier classification.
