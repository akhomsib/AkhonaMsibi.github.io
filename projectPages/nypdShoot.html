NYPD Shooting Incident Analysis

Tools: R

This project developed several time-series models to understand the temporal and spatial patterns, demographic trends, and underlying factors associated with shooting incidents in New York City. These sought to identify key vulnerabilities, potential causes, and correlations, which could offer actionable insights to policy makers, law enforcers, and communities at large. From this, seasonal patterns were found, with more shooting incidents occurring during the Summer and in warmer weather.

This analysis also found that increased levels of unemployment coincided with more shooting incidents in the area, highlighted by a large increase in these incidents post-Covid. This predictive multivariate model allowed for forecasting of incidents for easier preparation for high volume time periods, and strategizing for future staffing needs.

Achievements

  • Established seasonal patterns with more shooting incidents occurring during the Summer and a positive causal relationship with warmer weather through trend and multivariate analyses.
  • Discovered positive relationship between unemployment and police shooting incidents through extensive multivariate analysis, highlighted by the sharp increase in incidents within the area since the start of the covid pandemic.
  • Improved accuracy in predicting future shooting occurrences using an ARMAX multivariate time-series model that incorporated several key indicators for enhanced forecasting and strategic planning.
